# Heirloom — 12-Week Content Calendar (Jun 1 → Aug 23, 2026)

> **Voice gate:** every post here sits under the headline *"Start your family's thousand-year thread."*
> Canonical voice = [marketing/automation/src/voice.ts](marketing/automation/src/voice.ts) +
> [marketing/PLAYBOOK.md](marketing/PLAYBOOK.md) §1. Time-locks are the magic; continuity-beyond-the-
> company is the moat; the *family* is the subject, never the brand. **We are not Storyworth** — no
> "give them a year of stories," no "buy time instead of flowers," never the word *legacy*.
>
> **Engine link:** these 12 weeks correspond to ISO weeks **23–34** in
> [themes.ts](marketing/automation/src/themes.ts). Those entries were realigned to this voice in the
> same pass (they previously used the forbidden gift-a-year framing). The autonomous engine posts
> daily at 14:00 UTC; this doc is the human-readable editorial layer + the hero copy per week.
>
> **One seasonal peak in window:** Father's Day, **Sun Jun 21 2026** (Week 3). Grandparents Day
> (Sun Sep 13) lands just past Week 12 — prep begins in Weeks 11–12.

## How to use this
- Each week has: **the moment** (why this week), the **hero post** (fully drafted, platform-agnostic
  seed), **per-platform angles**, the **share-worthy time-lock beat**, and the **CTA**.
- The hero post is what `generate.ts` would produce as the source; `variants.ts` adapts per platform.
  Drafted heroes here can be pasted straight into queue mode or used to sanity-check the generator.
- Posting cadence per PLAYBOOK §4: 1 source/day, fanned to IG, TikTok, Pinterest, FB, LinkedIn, X,
  Bluesky. Direct-API platforms post automatically; X/TikTok/Threads/YT go via the queue.
- Hashtag ceiling: **5 max** (IG/Pinterest none), lowercase, from the approved set in voice.ts.

## WEEK 1 · Jun 1–7 · *The thread is live*  (ISO w23)
**The moment.** Soft-launch energy. Establish the world-first idea before the Father's Day push.
**Hero post.**
> Your great-granddaughter is going to ask why nobody recorded her grandfather's voice.
> A thread is the answer. One family writes into it — today, and in 2120. You can lock an entry
> so it doesn't open until her 18th birthday. Even we can't read it until then.
> Open a thread. The first entry is the hardest one.
**Per-platform angles.**
- **X / Bluesky:** the one-sentence world-first — "A family archive that's append-only, multi-generational, and time-locked. Started today, read in 2120." No hashtags.
- **TikTok/Reels:** talking-head — a person reading the first line of an entry they locked for a child who isn't born yet. First 6 words must land.
- **Pinterest:** "how to start a family history thread" — long-tail SEO, keyword-dense, no hashtags.
- **LinkedIn:** the failure-mode observation — every digital-legacy startup died and took the content with it; what an append-only, exportable, IPFS-pinned archive changes.
- **Facebook:** a tiny story — "the names in the photo nobody can identify anymore" — ending in a question.
**Time-lock beat.** Demo the Sealed Note → Unlock dissolve (a date "arriving" on screen). This is the share-worthy frame.
**CTA.** *Open a thread. Lock your first entry for whoever you want.* → `/signup`

## WEEK 2 · Jun 8–14 · *Dads are harder. Ask better.*  (ISO w24 → Father's Day prep)
**The moment.** One week out from Father's Day. Reframe from "buy dad a gift" to "ask dad the thing."
**Hero post.**
> He says he doesn't need anything. He means it. So don't buy a thing — ask a question.
> "Dad, what's the bravest thing you ever did that you've never told me?" Record the answer.
> Lock it in a thread your kids will open long after. The story outlasts the tie.
**Per-platform angles.**
- **TikTok:** "the one question that opens your dad up" — list-of-one, surprising.
- **Pinterest:** "questions to ask your dad before it's too late" (target #questionstoaskdad searchers).
- **LinkedIn:** why men of a certain generation don't volunteer their stories, and the structure that gets them out.
- **X:** "Don't buy your father a gift this year. Record one story. Lock it for your kids."
**Time-lock beat.** Lock dad's answer for *your* child's 18th — "the day your kid hears their grandfather's voice."
**CTA.** *Ask one question this week. Record it. Keep it forever.*

## WEEK 3 · Jun 15–21 · **FATHER'S DAY (Sun Jun 21)** — conversion peak  (ISO w25/seasonal)
**The moment.** The single biggest intent window in the quarter. Push hard but on-voice.
**Hero post (Father's Day morning).**
> Today, ask him one thing you've never asked. Not for a card — for the thread.
> "What did your own dad never tell you?" Two generations open up in one question.
> Record it on your phone. Put it somewhere it can't be lost — a thread your family keeps for good.
**Per-platform angles.**
- **Reels/TikTok (Sat + Sun):** real family member reading a real entry; raw, unpolished, 9–15s.
- **Facebook:** "phones out at the table isn't rude today — it's the record." Invite comments: *what did you ask him?*
- **X/Bluesky:** "A gift gets used up. A recorded story gets inherited."
- **Pinterest:** "Father's Day questions to ask dad" pin (evergreen, will earn for years).
**Time-lock beat.** The grandfather→grandchild two-generation lock — the emotional apex of the window.
**CTA.** *It's not too late. Open a thread today and record one answer.* → `/signup` (and `/founder` for the committed)

## WEEK 4 · Jun 22–28 · *Ask him about his dad*  (ISO w26, post-Father's-Day)
**The moment.** Convert the holiday's emotional residue into a habit, not a one-off.
**Hero post.**
> You called him on Sunday. Now ask the harder one: tell me about your father.
> Most of us know our grandparents as a handful of photos and a couple of sayings.
> One recorded answer this week, and your kids inherit a great-grandfather they'd never have met.
**Angles.** LinkedIn: the asymmetry of generational memory. Pinterest: "grandfather interview questions." X: the two-generation math.
**Time-lock beat.** Lock the grandfather story for the next child's birth.
**CTA.** *One question a week. By winter you'll have a thread.*

## WEEK 5 · Jun 29–Jul 5 · *The summer they were 16*  (ISO w27)
**The moment.** Summer nostalgia is a low-resistance entry point.
**Hero post.**
> Before they were "mom" or "dad," they were 16 and certain they had it figured out.
> Ask about that summer — the job, the car that barely ran, the heartbreak that felt permanent.
> Write it into the thread. The version of them you never met is the one your kids will love most.
**Angles.** TikTok: "ask your parent about the summer they were 16." Facebook: a first-job story + *what was yours?* Pinterest: "summer memory prompts for parents."
**Time-lock beat.** A "to my kids at 16" entry — locked until they reach the same age.
**CTA.** *Write one summer down before it blurs.*

## WEEK 6 · Jul 6–12 · *The recipe with no card*  (ISO w28)
**The moment.** Food = memory; the most-shared category in this niche.
**Hero post.**
> Her sauce had no recipe. It lived in her hands. When she's gone, it goes with her — unless
> you stand in the kitchen with her once, recording, and ask her to say each step out loud.
> That recording is the card. Put it in the thread where it can't be lost.
**Angles.** Reels: cooking-alongside, voice recording. Pinterest: "record family recipes" (huge search). FB: "whose recipe died with them?" X: "Her recipe with no card. Dad's voice on a 1997 tape. Save the ones still savable."
**Time-lock beat.** Lock the recipe + voice for a grandchild's first apartment.
**CTA.** *Record one recipe in their own voice this week.*

## WEEK 7 · Jul 13–19 · *Halfway through the year*  (ISO w29)

**Time-lock beat.** Show the append-only counter climbing — *entry 1,247* — the number descendants keep watching grow.
**CTA.** *Make it a habit. One entry a week.*

## WEEK 8 · Jul 20–26 · *Photos with no captions*  (ISO w30)
**The moment.** Everyone has the shoebox / the 4,000-photo camera roll.
**Hero post.**
> Your kids will inherit the JPEGs. They won't inherit who's in them.
> Pull up one old photo with your mom. Ask: who is everyone, and what happened right after this?
> Write it under the picture, in the thread. A captioned photo is a story. The rest is just pixels.
**Angles.** TikTok: "caption one old photo with your parent." Pinterest: "how to caption old family photos." FB: post a (consented) old photo, *tell me about yours.*
**Time-lock beat.** Attach the photo to an entry locked for a future descendant who'll recognize no one in it — until they read this.
**CTA.** *Caption one photo before the names are gone.*

## WEEK 9 · Jul 27–Aug 2 · *Where they came from*  (ISO w31)
**The moment.** Every family has a "how we got here" story; high emotional + SEO value.
**Hero post.**

**Angles.** LinkedIn: first-generation stories and what gets lost in two generations. Pinterest: "family immigration story interview." X: the "name changed at a desk" detail.
**Time-lock beat.** Lock the origin story as the thread's founding entry — visible to every future member.
**CTA.** *Record the crossing. It's chapter one.*

## WEEK 10 · Aug 3–9 · *What they believed*  (ISO w32)
**The moment.** Faith, doubt, change — the inner life we rarely ask about.
**Hero post.**
> You know what your parents did. Do you know what they believed — and where it changed?
> The thing they were sure of at 25 and unsure of at 60 is the most human thing about them.
> Ask gently. Write it down. Belief is the part of a person that photos never catch.
**Angles.** Facebook: "what did your parents believe that you didn't?" LinkedIn: how convictions evolve across a life. Bluesky: honest framing, no sell.
**Time-lock beat.** A "what I believe now, for you to read at 30" entry, locked.
**CTA.** *Ask one thing you've never asked.*

## WEEK 11 · Aug 10–16 · *Two generations back, already half-lost*  (ISO w33 → Grandparents Day prep)
**The moment.** Begin the Grandparents Day (Sep 13) runway. They're 80; the clock is real, said warmly.

> Your great-grandparents are already mostly gone — a name, a country, a single story retold

**CTA.** *Call your grandparent this week. Bring one question.*

## Cross-cutting mechanics (apply every week)
- **The Sealed Note → Unlock dissolve is the franchise visual.** Every Reel/TikTok should try to end on
  a date "arriving" and an entry opening. It's the one motion the brief lets be ceremonial (§1.5 D) — lean on it.
- **The append-only counter** (*entry 1,247*) is a recurring proof-without-copy. Show it climbing.
- **Always end with a small, specific action** (voice rule 11). Never "start your legacy journey."
- **Founder tier** (`/founder`, $999 lifetime) is the upsell for the most-moved viewers — surface it in
  Weeks 3, 9, 12 (high-emotion weeks), never as the lead.
- **UTM everything** to the share loops (Week 5 viral mechanics): `?utm_source=<platform>&utm_campaign=12wk_w<NN>`.

## Measurement (wire to metrics.ts)
Per week, track: signups, Founder conversions, `/inherit` link opens (the organic loop), and which
time-lock beat drove the most shares. Double down on the winning beat in the following quarter's calendar.
