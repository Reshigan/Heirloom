# The Heirloom Book

> A printed family volume a bloodline can order — the proof that the cloth outlives the company.
>
> Master line on the object: **Some things are meant to be kept.**
> Quiet durability proof-point, on the colophon only: *kept for as long as your family lasts.*

The Book is not a "print export." It is the physical form of the Cloth. When someone asks *"what
happens if Heirloom is gone in a hundred years?"* — the Book is the answer you can hold. It is the
single deliverable in the brand that needs no power, no account, no server, and no us. A family
prints it when a chapter of life closes (a birth, a passing, a fiftieth year, a child leaving home),
and it sits on a shelf doing the one thing the product promises: keeping the conversation going.

Everything below is built only from the authoritative brand system — its three honest materials
(woven structure, dyed thread, warm paper light), the Warp-and-Weft mark, the type stack (Fraunces /
Source Serif 4 / Inter / JetBrains Mono), the 10 named dyes, the ember-is-now rule, and the reverent
release lexicon. No new visual language is invented here.

## 1. The object

A premium, sewn, cloth-bound hardcover built to survive a century of being taken off a shelf. Nothing
about it should read as "print-on-demand." It reads as a family record that happens to have been
typeset beautifully.

| Property | Specification | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Format** | Royal octavo portrait, **156 × 234 mm** (6.14 × 9.21 in) trimmed | Hand-held like a real book, not a coffee-table photo dump. Tall measure protects the 66ch reading column. |
| **Extent** | 96 pp minimum → 480 pp maximum, in 16pp signatures | Smith-sewn signatures only; perfect-bound spines crack and shed pages in 20 years — disqualifying for a hundred-year object. |
| **Binding** | **Section-sewn (Smith-sewn), rounded & backed**, with a sewn-in cloth headband in the family's *founding dye* | The spine flexes flat-ish without breaking; the coloured headband is the first dye signal you see on the shelf. |
| **Cover boards** | 2.5mm greyboard, square-backed case | Rigid, archival, will not bow. |
| **Cover material** | **Buckram book cloth**, ground colour matched to theme: *ink* `#0b0907` (default) or *vellum* `#f6ecda` (light edition) | Buckram is the canonical "woven structure" material in physical form — it is literally cloth. Abrasion- and light-fast for archival shelving. |
| **Endpapers** | Uncoated 140gsm, printed with a **single full-bleed warp grid** at 8% bone on ink (or 8% ink on vellum) — the empty loom, before the family's threads are woven | The reader opens the cover into the warp itself. |
| **Text paper** | **Munken Pure Rough 120gsm**, FSC, acid-free, cream-white | Warm "paper light," not blue-white. Bulks well, takes Source Serif 4 cleanly, ages to amber gracefully instead of yellowing harshly. |
| **Plate paper (photos)** | Optional 16pp **Munken Lynx 170gsm** silk insert, tipped in | Photographs get their own signature so the matte reading paper never has to carry glossy ink. |
| **Head/tail bands & ribbon** | One **dyed ribbon marker** in the keeper's own dye | The keeper's thread, sewn into the spine, to hold their place. |
| **Edges** | Natural deckle on the fore-edge of the optional plate insert; clean trim on text | Tactile, archival, never gilded (gilt edges read as "trophy," not "record"). |
| **Slipcase (Keeper / Founder editions)** | Cloth-wrapped greyboard slipcase, blind-debossed mark on the spine end | Protects the foil cover; the everyday surface you touch. |

### Cover treatment — the Family Spectrum, in foil

The cover is the most ownable object in the whole brand because **no two families' covers are the
same.** It carries the *Family Spectrum* — the woven colour-bar of exactly the dyes this bloodline
contains — generated from real data, rendered as foil bands.

```
   FRONT COVER  (ink buckram, 156 × 234 mm)
   ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │                                            │
   │                                            │   top margin = 1.5× warp unit (48mm to first element)
   │                                            │
   │            ◳   ← the Warp-and-Weft mark    │   blind DEBOSS, centred on H-axis,
   │                (deboss, no foil)            │   ember knot the ONLY hot-foil hit (copper #e0a062)
   │                                            │
   │                                            │
   │              Heirloom                      │   copper foil wordmark, Fraunces, lowercase + capital H
   │                                            │
   │         THE  ▮▮▮▮▮ ▮▮▮ ▮▮▮▮▮  FAMILY        │   ← the FAMILY SPECTRUM: thin foil warp bands,
   │             keeper surname                 │     one band per dye the family holds, in family order.
   │                                            │     Foil-matched to each dye (see §8 foil map).
   │                                            │
   │                                            │
   │  ────────────────────────────────────     │   single bone/ink warp rule, full measure
   │  EST. 1979 · BLOODLINE OF __ · EDITION I   │   JetBrains Mono, uppercase, +0.12em, foil-blind
   │                                            │
   └──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Mark:** blind deboss (pressed into the cloth, no ink/foil) — *except* the **ember knot pip**, which
  is the one hot-foil hit in copper `#e0a062`. The mark's rule holds: one warm heartbeat at the join.
- **Wordmark "Heirloom":** copper foil, Fraunces opsz ~110 / weight ~360, lowercase except the capital H.
- **The Family Spectrum:** a row of slim foil warp bands, one per dye present in the bloodline, in the
  order members joined. A family of madder + indigo + walnut + weld reads as four bands — a coat of
  arms generated from who they actually are. Dyes appear here as foil bands (the cover is the *one*
  sanctioned place dyes become surface, because it is a coat of arms, not UI — and even here they are
  bands/margins, never fills behind type).
- **Spine:** wordmark + mark + the Spectrum repeated as a vertical warp ladder, so the dyes are visible
  on the shelf.
- **Reversed (vellum edition):** mark + wordmark in **ink deboss/foil on vellum cloth**, ember knot
  unchanged. Same artwork as the brand's reversed mark spec.

## 2. The page — typography in print

The page is the **warp grid made physical.** Content threads hang off a single left warp line; the
dye margin lives in that warp; the reading column never exceeds 66ch. Body is set large enough that a
grandmother reads it as a letter, not an app screen.

| Role | Typeface (print) | Size / leading | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chapter opener / voice | **Fraunces** opsz 110, wt 360, SOFT 0 WONK 0, tracking −0.01em | 28–40pt | Chapter titles, the Listener prompt, sealed/release openers. Never below 24pt. |
| Reading body / prose | **Source Serif 4** (variable) | **11pt / 16.5pt** (≈1.5 leading), 66ch measure | Every memory, letter, transcription. The hundred-year workhorse. |
| Chrome / captions / nav-of-page | **Inter** | 8.5pt / 12pt | Folios, running heads, photo captions, footnotes. |
| Archival stamp | **JetBrains Mono**, UPPERCASE, +0.12em, **tabular figures** | 7pt | Dates, generation counts, keeper lineage, entry IDs, colophon, page-foot watermark. |

**Print colour discipline (matches the screen rules exactly):**
- Text: warm near-black, **not** registration black. Print body at ink `#0b0907` as a rich-but-warm K-dominant build (see §8) so it reads as ink on cream, never cold.
- **Ember `#e0a062` is the live-present accent only** — it does NOT belong on archived pages. In print
  it appears on **exactly two surfaces:** the cover ember-knot foil, and the colophon's single ember
  rule. Past content is quiet — bone/ink + the member's dye. The page is calm.
- **Dyes are signal only:** each entry carries its author's dye as a **3px warp margin rule** down the
  left of the column, and the author's name set in the dye's AA-tuned text variant. Never as a fill,
  block, or background.

### Margins / grid

8px base rhythm scaled to print as a **4mm warp unit** (4px sub-grid → 2mm).

```
   RECTO PAGE GEOMETRY (156 × 234 mm)
   ├─ 22mm ─┤├──────── 66ch text column ────────┤├─ 16mm ─┤
   ▏        ▏                                      ▏
   ▏  warp  ▏  Running head (Mono, 7pt)            ▏
   ▏  line  ▏                                      ▏
   ▏  + 3px ▏  Source Serif 4 prose, 11/16.5,      ▏   inner (gutter) margin 22mm
   ▏  dye   ▏  ragged-right, 66ch max measure      ▏   to clear the sewn spine
   ▏  marg. ▏                                      ▏
   ▏        ▏                                      ▏
   ▏        ▏  ── author name (dye-text) ──        ▏
   ▏        ▏  PLACE · DATE  (Mono, 7pt)           ▏
   └ folio (Inter 8.5pt, outer)                    ┘
```

The dye warp margin sits in the **inner** margin on every page so that, when the book is open, both
facing pages show their threads running down toward the sewn spine — the cloth converging at the
binding, exactly the product metaphor.

## 3. The content model — Cloth → Book

The Book is a deterministic, append-only **render of the family's Cloth at a moment in time.** It maps
the product's real objects to print structures one-to-one. Nothing is invented and nothing is
auto-narrated — AI stays a labelled midwife (it may *suggest* a chapter title or clean a transcription
typo; it never writes a memory or speaks in anyone's voice).

| In the product | In the Book | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| **The Bloodline** | **Front matter: The Family Spectrum spread** | Endpaper warp grid → a double-page woven colour-bar of every member's dye with names, the keeper lineage, and generation count in Mono. |
| **A member** | A **thread** in the index + their dye throughout | Their entries always carry their dye warp margin + dye-text name. |
| **A memory** | A **page / entry block** | Source Serif 4 prose under a Mono `PLACE · DATE` stamp; author dye margin. |
| **A letter** | An entry block flagged **"a letter — to [name]"** in Fraunces | Letters get a slightly wider top margin (they breathe). |
| **A voice note** | Its **transcription**, marked `SPOKEN · [duration] · [date]` in Mono, with a small woven-cell glyph | The Book is the transcription of record; audio can't print, so the spoken word becomes the read word, clearly labelled as spoken. |
| **The Listener prompts the family answered** | **Call-and-response spreads** | The prompt set in Fraunces on the verso; the family's answers across years/members on the recto — the across-time engine, in print. |
| **A Sealed Note still sealed at print time** | A **reverent placeholder leaf** | A blank-but-present page: the release lexicon only — *"held until …"* / *"opens when …"* / *"for Maya, when she turns 30."* No body text, ever. The seal is honoured in print exactly as on screen. (See §4.) |
| **A Sealed Note already opened** | A normal entry block | Once opened in the product, it prints as any other entry, with a Mono note: `OPENED · [date] · PASSED ON TO YOU BY [name]`. |
| **The cloth / weave home plane** | **The colophon's woven endmark + the cover Spectrum** | The cloth itself becomes structure (binding, headband, warp grid), not an illustration. |

### Chaptering

The Book offers three structures the keeper chooses at order time; default is **Chronicle**.

1. **Chronicle (default)** — strict chronological weave. Chapters break on decade or on a life event
   the family already marked. Reads like the family's own century unfolding.
2. **By thread** — one chapter per member, in join order. Each opens on that member's dye and their
   *choosing-your-thread* moment.
3. **By dye / mood** — chapters grouped by the dye motif (joy, love, daily, travel, contemplation,
   reflection, achievement, record, grief, ending). The most editorial; the family's life arranged by
   feeling rather than time.

Chapter openers are always recto, on a fresh signature where possible, set in Fraunces with the
chapter's governing dye as a single warp rule and a Mono date-span stamp.

## 4. Sealed notes & the after-passing release — handled with dignity

The Book must never become the place a seal is broken or a death is announced. Two firm rules:

1. **A sealed note prints only as its reverent placeholder** — the fixed release lexicon, in Fraunces,
   on an otherwise empty leaf. No countdown, no lock glyph, no "deceased," no body text. It is a
   *promise on paper*, not a teaser.

   ```
   ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │                                            │
   │                                            │
   │            held until                      │   Fraunces 28pt, bone/ink
   │                                            │
   │       for Maya, when she turns 30          │   Fraunces 20pt
   │                                            │
   │                                            │
   │   ── one warp rule, the author's dye ──    │
   │                                            │
   │   SEALED BY GRANDMOTHER ROSE · WOVEN 2026  │   Mono 7pt, no ember
   │                                            │
   └──────────────────────────────────────────┘
   ```

2. **The after-passing release is never auto-printed by a routine.** A released letter enters the Book
   only when its *recipient* chooses to add it to the next edition — their choice, their timing,
   reflecting the screen rule that the recipient controls when it opens. When it does appear it is a
   normal entry block with the Mono line `PASSED ON TO YOU BY [name]` — reverent, no ember, no
   marketing, no app chrome. The dead-man's-switch and verification mechanics appear **nowhere** in the
   Book. Death is the architecture; the page only ever shows arrival-of-love.

## 5. Editioning & provenance — the colophon plate

Every Book is an **edition**, numbered and certified, because permanence has to be auditable. The
final leaf (or the slipcase-tipped vellum plate, for Keeper/Founder) is the **Provenance Plate** — the
"this is a record that outlives you" stamp made physical.

```
   THE COLOPHON / PROVENANCE PLATE  (verso, last leaf)
   ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │                                            │
   │              ◳   ← woven endmark           │   blind deboss, ember-knot foil
   │                                            │
   │   This volume is woven from the Cloth of   │   Source Serif 4, 11pt
   │   the ____ bloodline.                      │
   │                                            │
   │   ──────── one ember rule ────────         │   the ONLY ember on an interior page
   │                                            │
   │   EDITION ............... I of __          │   Mono 7pt, tabular figs, +0.12em
   │   THREADS WOVEN ......... 7                 │   member count
   │   ENTRIES ............... 318               │
   │   GENERATIONS ........... 3                 │
   │   SPAN .................. 1979 — 2026       │
   │   KEEPER LINEAGE ........ ROSE → AMARA →    │   the keepership chain
   │                           THIS KEEPER       │
   │   PRINTED ............... 2026-06-24        │
   │   COLLATION ............. SMITH-SEWN,       │
   │                           MUNKEN PURE       │
   │   CLOTH ID .............. HBL-00A1-7F3E     │   stable family/cloth identifier
   │   EDITION ID ............ ED-2026-0007      │   unique per printed copy
   │                                            │
   │   Some things are meant to be kept.        │   Fraunces 16pt
   │   Kept for as long as your family lasts.   │   Source Serif 4 11pt — the lone durability line
   │                                            │
   └──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **CLOTH ID** is stable across every edition a family ever prints (their permanent identifier);
  **EDITION ID** is unique per copy. Together they let a future descendant verify a physical Book
  against the family's record — provenance without surveillance.
- **Keeper lineage** prints the actual keepership chain, naming who held the thread, so the Book is
  self-documenting about its own custody.
- Numbered as `EDITION I of N` where N is the family's running count of editions ordered — an edition
  is a moment, not a print run, so even a single copy is "I."

## 6. The unboxing / heirloom ritual

The arrival is designed as the offline echo of *choosing your thread* — calm, reverent, never a
product-launch "wow unboxing." Nothing here uses urgency, ember-everywhere, or marketing chrome.

1. **The outer.** A plain recycled-board mailer, the warp grid blind-debossed on the lid, one Mono line:
   `SOMETHING WAS KEPT FOR YOUR FAMILY.` No logos screaming, no plastic.
2. **The wrap.** The Book sits in unbleached tissue tied with **a single thread in the family's founding
   dye** — the same dye on the headband and ribbon. The first physical thing the keeper touches is the
   family's own colour.
3. **The card.** A single Source Serif 4 letterpress card, no app screenshots, no "scan to…":
   > *This is your family's Cloth, held in your hands.*
   > *Add to it when a chapter closes. Pass it on when it's time.*
   > *Some things are meant to be kept.*
4. **The ribbon placed.** The keeper's dyed ribbon marker is left resting at the colophon — an
   invitation to read their own family's provenance first.
5. **The shelf moment.** The slipcase spine shows only the mark deboss and the dye headband — so on a
   shelf, years later, you find it by your family's colour, not by a brand.

There is **no QR code, no "activate," no app prompt** in the box. The Book's entire dignity is that it
needs nothing further. (A discreet Mono line inside the back board notes the CLOTH ID, for a keeper
who *wants* to print a later edition — pull, never push.)

## 8. Production / press notes (build-ready)

**Foil & ink map — the Family Spectrum and accents must hit real, repeatable colour:**

- **Wordmark + ember knot:** copper hot-foil matched to ember `#e0a062` (e.g. Kurz Luxor 220 copper
  range — proof to target). Ember knot is the only foil-glow in the system.
- **Dye foil bands (cover Spectrum):** map each dye's OKLCH token to a CMYK-safe foil/Pantone. The brand
  system flags **iron, woad, indigo** as the trio that *require a verified wet press proof* before run —
  do not approve from screen. madder/cochineal/kermes/saffron/weld/walnut/oakgall print within gamut.
- **Body ink:** warm near-black build approximating `#0b0907` — K-dominant rich black with a warm
  under-build (e.g. C20 M20 Y30 K100 territory, **proof to confirm warmth**) so type reads as ink, never
  cold registration black, on the cream Munken stock.
- **Vellum edition:** cover/foil reversed to ink on vellum cloth; interior unchanged.
- **Dye text variants in print:** use the AA-tuned `--dye-*-text` lightness for author names so they
  hold contrast on cream stock, same discipline as screen — names must clear legibility at 11pt.

**Binding/QA gates:**
- Smith-sewn only; reject any perfect-bound proof.
- Round & back; check spine flex at 96pp and at 480pp.
- Acid-free / FSC throughout; PUR/EVA glues only where sewing isn't load-bearing.
- Proof the warp-grid endpaper at 8% tint — it must whisper, not read as a pattern.
- Registration check on the dye warp margins: a 3px rule cannot drift into the gutter.

**Files / artwork lineage:**
- Cover artwork derives from the same Warp-and-Weft master used for the PWA mark and favicon — the
  deboss is the brand mark at print scale, not a redraw.
- The Family Spectrum is generated from the family's real dye set (the same data the screen header,
  share cards, and anniversary email footer read) — one source, three surfaces.
