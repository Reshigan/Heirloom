# Heirloom — Brand Book

> The definitive brand constitution. Contemporary, built from zero on the approved Brand
> System. This file supersedes every prior design language — the legacy "austere literary
> vault" (Cormorant, monastic 70%-empty, bare infinity glyph, glowing filament web) is
> retired and is **not** a starting point. What follows is binding. When code and this file
> disagree, fix the code.
>
> The only fixed truths above the brand are the **product truth** (Heirloom is a perpetual,
> append-only, multi-author, multi-generational family Thread, web at `heirloom.blue` + PWA)
> and the **frozen commercial facts** (Free $0 · Family $6.99/mo · $69/yr · Founder $249
> role-only, withdrawn from sale · ZAR localization untouchable). Everything visual and
> verbal in this book is ours to hold.

## 1 — Essence

**Held.**

Heirloom is the loom that keeps a family's voices woven together across generations. Every
life is a thread. The family is the cloth. And the cloth is held — for as long as the family
lasts.

That one word, *held*, is the whole company. It is what a grandmother feels when she reads it
and what a new parent feels when they write into it. It is the opposite of *stored*, *backed
up*, *secured*. We do not warehouse data. We hold a family's voices the way you hold a hand.

---

## 2 — Promise

> **You keep the conversation going. We keep it safe, and we make sure it reaches the people
> you meant it for — written, spoken, and passed on, for as long as your family lasts.**

The promise has two halves and both are load-bearing. The family does the keeping — they write,
they speak, they pass on. We do the holding — safe, reaching the right person, lasting. We never
claim to *be* the memory. We are the loom, not the cloth.

---

## 3 — Positioning & Tagline

**Master hero line — use this first, everywhere it counts:**

> ## Some things are meant to be kept.

It is the most emotional and the least boastful line we have. It assumes the reader already
knows what those things are. It never says *forever*, never says *legacy*, never raises its
voice.

**Supporting line — used after the hero, never as the hero:**

> Keep your family's story going — across generations.

**Durability proof-point — a single quiet line, never a headline:**

> Built to outlast everyone who uses it.

The in-market phrase *"Start your family's thousand-year thread"* is **demoted to a durability
proof-point** — it may appear once, low and quiet, as evidence of permanence. It is referenced
by the commercial vocabulary, so it is not deleted. But it is cold and it boasts, so it is never
the first thing anyone reads.

**The positioning, in one breath:** Heirloom is where a family keeps the conversation going
across generations — written, spoken, and passed on at exactly the right moment — held safe for
as long as the family lasts.

## 4 — Personality & Voice

The voice of someone alive at the kitchen table at golden hour. Warm, present-tense, second
person, **addressed to a real named person**. Concrete and sensory — hands, weather, food, a
kitchen, a name. Occasionally funny, occasionally aching, never bleak, never saccharine.
Plainspoken and certain without being clinical. Reverent at the sealed and release moments,
never funereal. Premium but unintimidating: a 34-year-old new parent and their 78-year-old
grandmother both find it beautiful and both find it easy.

We say **keep** and **pass on**. We never say *death*, *legacy data*, or *memorialize*.

### The seven voice rules

1. **Lead every sensitive moment with arrival-of-love, never loss.** *"For Maya, when she turns
   30"* — not *"after you're gone."* Death is the mechanism in the architecture; it is never the
   foreground emotion and never in acquisition copy.
2. **Present tense, second person, name a real person.** *"Tell your daughter what the kitchen
   smelled like"* beats *"preserve a memory."* Sensory and concrete over abstract.
3. **Use the kept-vocabulary** (§9). *Keep, pass on, held until, opens when, choose your thread.*
   Banish *memorialize, vault, secure your legacy, before it's too late.*
4. **No urgency, ever.** No countdowns, scarcity, streak guilt, FOMO, A/B pressure — and
   absolutely none near sealing, verification, release, or pricing. The thousand-year promise
   dies the instant the product behaves like quarterly-growth SaaS.
5. **No gamification of memory.** No badges, points, completion %, leaderboards. Reinforcement is
   relational and warm: *"Maya read your kitchen story,"* never *"+1, 3-day streak."*
6. **The verification speaks as calm caretaking.** *"A quiet note — just confirming all is well.
   No action needed if it is. Take your time."* Infrequent, reversible, reverent.
7. **AI is a labelled midwife, never a ventriloquist.** Gentle prompts, narration, dye
   suggestions — clearly marked as a tool. Never generate words or a voice impersonating a real
   (especially deceased) person.

### Do / Don't — example lines

| Surface | Say this | Never this |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | Some things are meant to be kept. | Secure your legacy before it's too late. |
| The Listener (daily) | What did your mother always say in the car? | You haven't written in 5 days. |
| Sealing a note | Held until Maya turns 30. | Locked. Releases in 4,015 days. ⏳ |
| Release (after passing) | Someone left this for you. It opens when you're ready. | Deceased member's memorial vault is now unlocked. |
| Verification (DMS) | A quiet note — just confirming all is well. No action needed if it is. | Confirm you are alive within 7 days or your account will be released. |
| Reinforcement | Your grandmother read your kitchen story. | +10 pts! 3-day streak! 🔥 |
| Social CTA | Ask someone tonight what their childhood bedroom smelled like. | Sign up now — limited spots! |
| Pricing | Family — $6.99/mo. Everyone in. | Upgrade now or lose your memories! |
| Onboarding | Choose your thread. | Pick your profile colour. |
| AI assist | A prompt to help you start — your words, always. | We wrote this memory in your grandfather's voice. |

## 5 — Distinctive Asset System

Seven assets do the recognition work. No icon library, no decorative emoji.

1. **The Warp-and-Weft mark.** A single structural over-under woven cell — three warps, two
   wefts, one ember knot at the held centre. Never a glowing node or filament web. It is
   ownable because it is the product's own data model — many threads, one cloth — made into a
   glyph. (Full law in §8.)
2. **Your colour for life.** At joining, each member chooses one of the ten named natural dyes.
   It becomes their name colour and their 3px left warp-margin thread, everywhere, forever. The
   onboarding ritual is literally *"choosing your thread."* Nobody else has this memory hook.
3. **The Family Spectrum.** The woven colour-bar of the exact dyes a specific family contains —
   a coat of arms generated from real data. It heads their Book cover (foil bands), their share
   cards, and the anniversary email footer. Every family's brand is literally theirs alone.
4. **Dyes named, not numbered.** *Madder, cochineal, kermes, saffron, weld, walnut, oakgall,
   woad, indigo, iron* — pigments with centuries of meaning instead of hex codes. A verbal and
   chromatic asset at once.
5. **The Listener.** One quiet daily prompt — the signature content unit across product, social,
   and email. Also a call-and-response engine that resurfaces a real family member's past entry
   to answer across time: *"Your grandmother wrote about this kitchen in 1979 — what do you
   remember?"*
6. **The mono archival stamp.** Dates, generation counts, keeper lineage, thread IDs — set in
   uppercase tracked mono. The same *"this is a record that outlives you"* watermark from the
   website footer to the printed Book colophon.
7. **The reverent release lexicon.** A fixed, gentle vocabulary set in the display serif —
   *held until, opens when, for Maya when she turns 30, passed on to you by.* Dignity rendered as
   a repeatable, designed asset. No countdowns, locks, or skulls.

## 6 — Visual System

Three honest materials: **woven structure, dyed thread, warm paper light.**

### 6.1 Space — the warp grid

Layout is a literal **warp grid**. An **8px base rhythm** (4px sub-grid). Content threads hang
off a **left warp line**. A single reading column, **max 66ch measure** for prose. Body type is
**≥17px everywhere** — multigenerational legibility is non-negotiable.

Negative space is structure, but **inhabited**: target **~55–60% empty** — warm and tactile,
never the old monastic 70%-empty unreadable vault. The 3D cloth/weave surface remains the home
plane; UI sits on a **warm paper plane above it** with generous quiet margins.

Material references stay abstract and premium — real woven-cloth macro photography and a genuine
warp/weft motion language. **Never** yarn balls, knitting needles, samplers, cartoon spools,
glassmorphism, conic-gradient mesh, animated noise, glowing filament webs, or AI "organic blob"
art.

### 6.2 Type

Two variable serifs, one sans, one mono. **Cormorant is dropped entirely** — it is the dated
vault tell.

| Role | Family | Settings | Where |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Display / Voice** | **Fraunces** (variable) | `opsz` ~110–120 at hero · weight ~360 · `SOFT` 0 · `WONK` 0 · tracking −0.01em | Hero lines, the Listener prompt, sealed/release copy, chapter openers. Never below its ~24px optical floor. Never running body. |
| **Reading / Prose / Inputs** | **Source Serif 4** (variable) | 17px / 1.6 | The memory-body workhorse — so a hundred-year-old letter reads like a letter, not an app. |
| **UI chrome** | **Inter** (variable) | tall x-height, calm | Buttons, nav, form labels, small product UI. |
| **Archival / Metadata** | **JetBrains Mono** | UPPERCASE · +0.12em tracking · tabular numerals | Dates, generation counts, keepership roles, entry IDs, the colophon, the OG footer stamp. Used sparingly. |

Type scale is fluid (clamp-based) — ~1.5 ratio at display, ~1.2 in body.

**Wordmark:** *Heirloom* in Fraunces, `opsz` ~110, weight ~360, all lowercase except a capital
`H`, tracking −0.01em. The two adjacent `o`s may optionally render as two small woven cells
echoing the mark — a quiet, never-required flourish.

### 6.5 The hierarchy rule

**Ember = NOW** (the live present, the call to write, the mark's knot). **Dye = WHO** (the family
member). **Past and sealed content is quiet bone/dye** — never ember. This single rule governs
product, email, social, and Book. If you are unsure whether something earns ember, it does not.

## 7 — Motion

One calm spring, physics-based — **stiffness ~170, damping ~26** — for entrances and layout.
Micro-states reduce to a **220ms ease**. **Threads settle; they never bounce.** Durations cluster
at **180 / 360 / 720 / 1400ms**.

Every motion carries information:

- A thread being **woven into the warp** = a new entry or member joining.
- A weft **folding under** = a note sealing.
- On load, the hero cloth **draws one ember weft across** (~1400ms), then rests.

**Nothing loops forever.** The cloth breathes once and stops.

**The Seal gesture** is the one moment of deliberate ceremony: a slow **press-and-hold / fold**
that feels like pressing wax — gravity in the hand, not in a warning banner. No ember urgency, no
countdown.

`prefers-reduced-motion` is **fully honoured** — sections appear without weaving; the cloth
renders settled. No spinners anywhere; loading is the hairline **ProgressHair**. No toasts; status
is inline.

## 8 — Mark Usage Law

The **Warp-and-Weft mark**: a single woven cell — structural, never a glowing node or filament
web. Master geometry (viewBox `0 0 192 192`):

- **Ground:** solid ink `#0b0907`, full bleed.
- **Three vertical warps:** bone `#f2e6d0`, round-cap stroke 9.6px, at `x = 64, 96, 128`, each
  running `y = 28 → 164`.
- **Two horizontal wefts:** bone, round-cap stroke 9.6px, at `y = 72` and `y = 120`. Each weft
  **interlaces** — a left segment `x=44→~88` (in front of the `x=64` warp), a ~16px gap across
  `x=96`, then a right segment `~104→148` (in front of the `x=128` warp). The **centre warp
  visibly crosses OVER both wefts** at `x=96`; the outer warps pass under. A true over-under 3×2
  cell.
- **The ember knot** at the held centre `(96,96)`: a soft radial halo `r=40` in ember `#e0a062` at
  ~0.4 alpha, behind a solid ember pip `r=12` (`#f0c074` core → `#e0a062`). This is the **only**
  colour in the mark and the **only** "glow" — a single warm heartbeat at the join, never a lens
  flare.

**Favicon** (viewBox `0 0 48 48`): simplified so the interlace still reads at 16px — two warps at
`x=18, 30` (stroke 3.2px, `y=8→40`) and one weft at `y=24` (stroke 3.2px) passing in front of the
left warp, gapping across centre, in front of the right warp — an over-under cross — plus the
ember pip `r=3` at `(24,24)` with a faint `r=8` halo. Reads as a woven knot, never a generic hash
or `#`.

**Maskable tile:** ink bleeds to the edge; the entire weave + ember knot lives inside the inner
80% safe circle, geometrically centred so the ember pip sits at tile centre and survives the iOS
squircle and the Android circle masks.

**Reversed** (on vellum): warps/wefts in ink `#0b0907`, ember knot unchanged — for the light
theme and the Book cover deboss.

**Lockup:** *Heirloom* in Fraunces (`opsz` ~110, weight ~360, lowercase but capital `H`, tracking
−0.01em). Horizontal lockup = mark tile at left, mark height = cap-height of the `H`, optical gap
≈ 0.6× cap-height between mark and wordmark.

### Mark do-not list
- No glowing radial node, converging filament web, or "cosmic loom" mark.
- Never recolour the warps/wefts to a dye. The mark is bone-on-ink (or ink-on-vellum) + the one
  ember knot, always.
- Never add a second glow, gradient mesh, bevel, or drop shadow.
- Never stretch, rotate, or outline the mark.
- Never substitute the bare `∞` or any icon-library glyph.

Shipped assets: `brand/mark/heirloom-mark-192.svg`, `brand/mark/heirloom-favicon-48.svg`,
`brand/mark/heirloom-maskable-512.svg`, `brand/mark/heirloom-lockup-vellum.svg`.

## 10 — Sensitive Surfaces (handle with dignity)

- **Sealing** is rendered as cloth — a folded weft, a thread woven in — with a mono date label.
  Never a padlock, hourglass, or countdown. The ceremony is the press-and-hold gesture; the
  dignity is the fixed reverent copy.
- **The dead-man's-switch / verification** speaks as calm caretaking, is infrequent, and is always
  reversible: *"A quiet note — just confirming all is well. No action needed if it is. Take your
  time."*
- **The after-passing release email** is the most carefully made object in the system: reverent,
  no marketing, no app chrome, **no ember**. The recipient controls **when** they open it.
  *"Someone left this for you. It opens now."*
- **Pricing** is stated plainly and never pressured: Free $0 · Family $6.99/mo or $69/yr · Founder
  $249 (role only, withdrawn from sale). ZAR localization is untouchable. No A/B pressure on price,
  ever.

## 11 — The Absolute Do-Not List

1. No countdown timers, scarcity, streaks, FOMO, or urgency anywhere — categorically forbidden
   near sealing, the dead-man's-switch, release, and pricing.
2. Never lead with death, loss, tombstones, hourglasses, padlocks, "deceased," "memorialize," or
   "before it's too late." Death lives in the architecture, never the foreground.
3. No glowing radial node, converging filament web, or sci-fi "cosmic loom" mark.
4. No glassmorphism, frosted/blurred translucency, conic-gradient mesh, animated noise, 3D bevels,
   or AI "organic blob" shapes.
5. No Cormorant Garamond; never set running body in the display serif or drop it below ~24px.
6. No dye colours as fills, buttons, or backgrounds — signal only (3px warp margin + name text).
7. No icon libraries or decorative emoji; the mark and the mono stamp carry recognition.
8. No avatar circles / `rounded-full` chips, no loading spinners (use ProgressHair), no toasts
   (inline status only).
9. No gamification of memory — no badges, points, completion %, leaderboards, or read-receipts
   framed as performance.
10. No performance pressure at the Composer — no audience counters, no AI quality-scoring, no
    "great memories get more reads."
11. No AI ventriloquism — never generate words or a voice impersonating a real or deceased person.
12. Never A/B-pressure or alter the frozen pricing; ZAR localization is untouchable.
13. No stock photography in email; no marketing chrome or ember on the after-passing release email.
14. Never reintroduce the austere 70%-empty monastic vault or tiny unreadable type.
